# SME AI Automation

> The market Qualia actually serves. Small-to-medium businesses (5–200 employees) who can't hire a dev team but have clearly-defined repetitive work worth €500–€5,000/month to automate.

## Characteristics of a good SME AI client

- **One clear job to automate.** Invoice entry, lead follow-up, appointment booking, product lookup, call answering. If the client can't name it in one sentence, don't take the project.
- **Existing pain they'll pay for now.** "We'd save 20 hours a week" beats "this would be cool."
- **Owner-operator or ops lead makes decisions.** Committee-driven procurement doesn't close at our speed.
- **Willing to publish a voice / chat under their brand.** Clients who want to "white-label" a third party aren't SMEs, they're resellers.

## Anti-patterns that drain teams

- **"Build me a chatbot for my website."** No. Chatbots without a specific job churn in <90 days. Pitch a voice agent with one role instead.
- **Freemium AI features.** SMEs don't upsell themselves. Flat retainer or don't take it.
- **"Train a custom model on our data."** 99% of the time, prompt + RAG clears the bar. Custom fine-tuning is a 2026 enterprise sell, not SME.
- **Long RFPs.** If the client wants a 40-page proposal, they're not buying an agency, they're buying a consultant. Hand them off.

## Playbook: 4-week SME delivery

1. **Week 1** — discovery + one paid diagnostic meeting. Produce a one-page scope and an MRR quote.
2. **Week 2** — staging deploy, brand + voice tuning, client tests on own phone / device.
3. **Week 3** — go-live, monitor 48h, iterate on transcript / chat logs.
4. **Week 4** — first retrospective, lock retainer amount, hand over admin view.

Most Qualia deployments hit this cadence. Sakani is the outlier (12k JOD / 4 milestones) because it's a full mobile app, not an SME agent.
